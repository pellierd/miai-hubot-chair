## 🎯 Project Overview
In the context of Industry 4.0, reprogramming industrial robots remains a costly and complex task requiring expert intervention. The **HUBOT Chair** proposes an innovative and interdisciplinary framework based on **Programming by Demonstration (PbD)**, enabling non-expert operators to teach robots through intuitive kinesthetic interaction.

Our research combines expertise from robotics, artificial intelligence, and cognitive ergonomics to develop a novel co-manipulation model that facilitates natural human–robot collaboration without requiring users to adapt their gestures.

---

## 🔬 Core Innovation Pillars
* **Fluid Co-Manipulation:** Designing a system that allows natural, stable, and intuitive physical collaboration.
* **Few-Shot Learning:** Creating learning algorithms capable of extracting and generalizing trajectories from a limited number of demonstrations (< 10).
* **Task Semantic Modeling:** Integrating symbolic task representations (PDDL-based) with low-level robot control to generate explainable actions.
* **Ergonomics & Usability:** Establishing a psycho-ergonomic framework to analyze physical and cognitive load to improve the operator work experience.
