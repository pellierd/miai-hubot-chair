# Research Pillars (Work Packages)

The project combines expertise from robotics, artificial intelligence, and cognitive ergonomics across four complementary Work Packages:

* **WP1: Robust and Natural Co-Manipulation** Developing an advanced control framework that ensures fluid, stable, and natural physical interaction between human operators and robots during contact-rich tasks (such as clipsable component assembly).
* **WP2: Trajectory Learning by Demonstration** Designing low-data, incremental learning algorithms capable of extracting and generalizing motion policies from a very limited number of demonstrations (fewer than 10 per gesture).
* **WP3: Task Semantic Learning and Planning (Neuro-Symbolic Approach)** Utilizing a hybrid neuro-symbolic architecture to link continuous trajectory data with high-level symbolic representations (PDDL). This allows the robot to understand goals, plan action sequences, and provide explainable decisions to the operator.
* **WP4: Ergonomic Evaluation and Acceptability** Establishing a psycho-ergonomic evaluation framework to analyze physical, cognitive, and psychosocial workloads, ensuring that collaborative systems are user-friendly, inclusive, and promote sustainable work practices.
