# Welcome to the HUBOT Chair Website

The HUBOT Chair (Human-Centered Programming by Demonstration for Collaborative Industrial Robotics) is an innovative, interdisciplinary research project supported by the MIAI Cluster IA. 

Our mission is to redefine how humans collaborate with industrial robots by developing Programming by Demonstration (PbD) frameworks that are intuitive, safe, and truly human-centered.

---

## The Grand Challenge
In the context of Industry 4.0, reprogramming industrial robots remains a costly and complex task requiring expert intervention. Traditional programming approaches fail to provide the flexibility needed for modern production environments characterized by high variability and customization.

HUBOT bridges this technical bottleneck by empowering non-expert operators to teach robots complex tasks through intuitive kinesthetic interaction (guiding the robot by hand). This transforms the robot from a rigid machine into a natural, collaborative instrument that supports the worker.

---

## Research Pillars (Work Packages)

The project combines expertise from robotics, artificial intelligence, and cognitive ergonomics across four complementary Work Packages:

* **WP1: Robust and Natural Co-Manipulation**
  Developing an advanced control framework that ensures fluid, stable, and natural physical interaction between human operators and robots during contact-rich tasks (such as clipsable component assembly).
* **WP2: Trajectory Learning by Demonstration**
  Designing low-data, incremental learning algorithms capable of extracting and generalizing motion policies from a very limited number of demonstrations (fewer than 10 per gesture).
* **WP3: Task Semantic Learning and Planning (Neuro-Symbolic Approach)**
  Utilizing a hybrid neuro-symbolic architecture to link continuous trajectory data with high-level symbolic representations (PDDL). This allows the robot to understand goals, plan action sequences, and provide explainable decisions to the operator.
* **WP4: Ergonomic Evaluation and Acceptability**
  Establishing a psycho-ergonomic evaluation framework to analyze physical, cognitive, and psychosocial workloads, ensuring that collaborative systems are user-friendly, inclusive, and promote sustainable work practices.

## A Connected Ecosystem

The project leverages a robust interdisciplinary team and cross-institutional collaborations:
* **UGA / LIG**: The Laboratoire d'Informatique de Grenoble at the Université Grenoble Alpes.
* **UCA / Institut Pascal**: The Institut Pascal at the Université Clermont Auvergne.

Our fundamental research is directly validated on industrial demonstrators for assembly and packaging through active partnerships with international academic institutions and non-academic world leaders such as Stäubli and Kassow Robots.
